Jack's Island — Aventures & Expression écrite

English · 4e — PP / Past Simple / Emotions
📬 1. Jack's Letter — Read & Discover
Lis cette lettre de Jack, qui vit sur une île depuis plusieurs mois. Les mots en jaune sont au Present Perfect. Les mots en bleu sont au Past Simple.

My dear family,


I have been here for two months now and I really like my new life on the island. Last week on my birthday, my new best friend told me "Jack, you're 21 now, you're a real man". We laughed.


I have learnt a lot of things since I have arrived. I feel like one of the natives. I have managed to build a fire using just two sticks of wood. I have caught fish. In a way, I feel like I have always lived here.


Yesterday, my friend took me on his little boat and we went down the river for about 4 miles. We saw crocodiles and strange birds. It was scary.


I hope to hear from you soon,
Your son.

💡 As-tu remarqué ? Jack utilise deux temps différents :
  • Le Present Perfect (jaune) → pour parler de ses expériences depuis qu'il est sur l'île
  • Le Past Simple (bleu) → pour raconter une anecdote précise (hier, la semaine dernière)
✅ 2. Quick Grammar Reminder
📚 Tu as déjà étudié ces temps en détail dans d'autres cours !
Voici juste un rappel rapide pour lire la lettre de Jack. Pour les formules complètes → voir Present Perfect & Sydney et Simple Past vs Present Perfect.

⚡ Rappel éclair — Present Perfect

Subject + HAVE / HAS + Past Participle

→ Expériences de vie (sans date précise) : I have caught fish.
→ Action qui dure depuis le passé : I have been here for two months.
→ Avec : for / since / just / already / never / not yet / ever

⚡ Rappel éclair — Past Simple

Regular: verb + ED  |  Irregular: special form (go→went, see→saw, take→took)

→ Action terminée à un moment précis et connu : Yesterday, we went down the river.
→ Avec : yesterday / last week / ago / in 2020 / last night

⏱️ 3. Key adverbs with the Present Perfect
FOR
pendant (une durée)
I have been here for two months.
→ dit combien de temps
SINCE
depuis (un point de départ)
I have learnt a lot since I arrived.
→ dit depuis quand
JUST
juste, tout à l'heure
He has just caught a fish.
→ ça vient de se passer
ALREADY
déjà
I have already built a fire.
→ c'est fait, plus tôt que prévu
NEVER
jamais
I have never seen a crocodile.
→ expérience nulle
NOT YET
pas encore
I have not replied yet.
→ action prévue mais non faite
EVER
déjà (dans une question)
Have you ever seen a crocodile?
→ expérience dans la vie
ALWAYS
toujours
I feel like I have always lived here.
→ depuis très longtemps
🧠 Astuce FOR vs SINCE — comment ne pas confondre :

FOR + durée (combien de temps ?) : for two months / for a week / for ages / for 3 years
SINCE + point de départ (depuis quand ?) : since January / since I arrived / since 2020 / since his birthday

💡 Test : si tu peux répondre à "combien de temps ?" → FOR. Si tu réponds à "depuis quand ?" → SINCE.

📬 La lettre de Jack — Ce qu'elle contient

  • Type : une lettre (pas un journal intime / not a diary)
  • Auteur : Jack, 21 ans, sur une île depuis 2 mois
  • Destinataire : sa famille ("My dear family")
  • Expériences (PP) : a appris à faire du feu, à pêcher, se sent comme un natif
  • Anecdote (Past Simple) : hier, son ami l'a emmené en bateau → ils ont vu des crocodiles
  • Ton : positif, aventurier, mais parfois effrayant ("It was scary")

🆚 PP vs Past Simple — L'essentiel

  • I have caught fish. → expérience, pas de date → Present Perfect
  • Yesterday, we went down the river. → date précise → Past Simple
  • I have been here for two months. → durée qui continue → PP + for
  • I have learnt a lot since I arrived. → depuis un événement → PP + since

🔑 Verbes irréguliers clés

  • go → went → gone
  • see → saw → seen
  • take → took → taken
  • catch → caught → caught
  • build → built → built
  • tell → told → told

😊 Emotions à connaître pour l'éval

  • scared / frightened → effrayé(e) — "It was scary."
  • excited → enthousiaste
  • proud → fier(e)
  • amazed → émerveillé(e)
  • worried → inquiet(e)

Phrases utiles : I feel like... / It was scary. / I couldn't believe it! / In a way, ...

📓 Feelings in context — Two diary entries
Lis ces deux journaux intimes. Les mots en vert expriment la joie / bonheur. Les mots en rouge expriment la tristesse / colère.

😊 25th July — A great day!

It's been a wonderful day. We haven't been so happy or felt so good in a long time. The sun has been shining all day. We've had so much fun together! It's been so great to go sailing on the lake. Everyone enjoyed it! We've had great laughs with Peter. I can't believe he was afraid of water-skiing. Great success! Conclusion: AWESOME DAY!

😠 23rd August — A terrible day!

I hate Kate! She's ruined my day! She's always been so mean to me! I feel terrible! This is the worst day of my life! She's said awful stuff to John. I am really scared and worried. I can't trust this girl. She's horrible! I am so down tonight. I'm fed up with everything. Conclusion: BAD DAY!
